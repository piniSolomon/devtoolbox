About Meta Tag Generator

Meta tags provide metadata about your HTML page. They don't appear on the page itself but are used by search engines, social media platforms, and browsers to understand and display your content.

Essential Meta Tags for SEO

  • Title: The most important on-page SEO element. Appears in search results and browser tabs. Keep it under 60 characters.
  • Description: Appears as the snippet in search results. 150-160 characters ideal. Include your target keyword naturally.
  • Canonical: Prevents duplicate content issues by specifying the "official" URL of the page.
  • Robots: Controls whether search engines index the page and follow its links.
  • Viewport: Essential for mobile responsiveness. Standard value: width=device-width, initial-scale=1.0

Open Graph & Twitter Cards

  • og:title, og:description: Control how your page appears when shared on Facebook, LinkedIn, WhatsApp, etc.
  • og:image: The preview image shown in social shares. Recommended: 1200×630 pixels.
  • twitter:card: Controls Twitter's card format. summary_large_image shows a large image preview.

FAQ

Are meta keywords still important?

No. Google has officially confirmed that it ignores the meta keywords tag for ranking purposes. However, some other search engines and tools may still use them. Including them doesn't hurt, but don't rely on them for SEO.

What's the ideal title length?

Under 60 characters. Google displays up to ~580 pixels of the title in desktop search results (about 50-60 characters). Titles that are too long get truncated with "...".
